üéØ CEFR PRE-A1 "Can Do" Statements

Students at PRE-A1 level can:

  • Listening: Recognize basic greetings (hello, goodbye) and single words for common objects, colors, and numbers 1-10
  • Reading: Understand simple, isolated words and phrases related to familiar topics (greetings, numbers, basic family vocabulary)
  • Speaking: Produce basic greetings and introduce self with name (highly controlled, memorized phrases)
  • Writing: Write isolated words from a familiar list (with visual support); copy simple greetings and numbers
  • Grammar: Recognize present tense "be" verb (I am, You are, He/She is, It is) in context
  • Vocabulary: Know 500-700 basic words: greetings, numbers 1-10, common objects, animals, colors, simple family relationships

Teacher Guidance & Assessment Notes

Assessment Strategy:
This PRE-A1 test assesses foundational English competencies aligned with CEFR breakthrough level. The test covers five interconnected domains essential for absolute beginners: social greetings, numeracy, object recognition, family relationships, and basic actions.
Question Distribution:
‚Ä¢ Section 1 (Greeting): Tests social formulas and "be" verb (4 questions + 1 fixed phrase)
‚Ä¢ Section 2 (Numbers): Tests cardinal numbers 1-10 and sequencing (5 questions)
‚Ä¢ Section 3 (Objects/Colors): Tests object and color vocabulary with semantic relationships (5 questions)
‚Ä¢ Section 4 (Family/Animals): Tests family terminology and animal knowledge (5 questions)
‚Ä¢ Section 5 (Verbs): Tests basic action verbs and verb conjugation (5 questions)
Difficulty Progression:
‚úì Easy (Q1-5, Q11-15): Direct vocabulary and recognition
‚úì Medium (Q6-10, Q16-20): Application and reasoning
‚úì Challenging (Q21-25): Grammar and contextual understanding
Common Errors to Watch:
‚Ä¢ Confusion between "am/is/are" - recommend explicit "be" verb practice
‚Ä¢ Third person singular "-s" omission - common across L1 backgrounds
‚Ä¢ Family relationship logic errors - use family tree diagrams for remediation
‚Ä¢ Verb-object collocation mistakes - teach phrasal chunks, not isolated words
Remediation Recommendations by Score Range:
90-100%: Advance to A1. Consider challenge activities (simple dialogues, basic writing).
80-89%: A1 ready. Monitor "be" verb and verb conjugation during A1 instruction.
70-79%: Conditional A1 entry. Provide 1-2 weeks targeted PRE-A1 review before A1.
50-69%: Extend PRE-A1 instruction. Focus on vocabulary retention and grammar fundamentals.
<50%: Recommend individual support or alternative teaching method. May need L1 support initially.

üìö Recommended Follow-Up Activities

  • For Advanced Students (90%+): Introduce A1 speaking tasks (self-introductions), simple present tense expansion, basic questions and answers
  • For Proficient Students (80-89%): Reinforce through dialogues, listening to simple stories, controlled writing exercises
  • For Borderline Students (70-79%): Intensive vocabulary drills, grammar pattern practice, peer learning activities
  • For Struggling Students (50-69%): Multisensory vocabulary learning, repetition through songs/games, visual support materials
  • For At-Risk Students (<50%): Individualized review, alternative instruction methods, L1 clarification as needed
