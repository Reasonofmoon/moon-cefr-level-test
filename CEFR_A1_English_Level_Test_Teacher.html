üéØ CEFR A1 "Can Do" Statements

Students at A1 level can:

  • Listening: Recognize basic words and expressions from familiar topics; understand simple, clear speech at normal speed
  • Reading: Understand simple texts on familiar topics (notes, postcards, simple stories); recognize familiar words and phrases
  • Speaking: Produce simple, memorized phrases; introduce themselves with name and basic information; answer simple questions
  • Writing: Write simple messages, notes, and basic sentences; describe familiar people and objects using basic vocabulary
  • Grammar: Use present simple "to be" and simple present tense; recognize basic question forms; understand simple word order
  • Vocabulary: Know 1000-1500 words: common objects, family, numbers, colors, basic verbs, daily routines

Common Errors & Remediation

Typical Problem Areas:

  • Third Person Singular -s (Q28): Students often omit -s ending. Remediate with pattern drills and contracted speech recognition.
  • Past Tense (Q31): Confusion between base form, -ed ending, and irregular forms. Use timeline visuals and regular/irregular verb tables.
  • Auxiliary Verbs (Q30): Students confuse Do/Does in questions. Practice with question word cards and transformation exercises.
  • BE in Different Forms (Q29): is/are/was/were confusion. Use tense markers and repetition with temporal adverbials.
  • Question Words (Q33): What/Where/When confusion. Create exercise sheets with contextual clues for each question word.

Recommended Follow-Up by Score Range:

90%+: Provide extension activities: writing tasks, peer teaching, introduction to A2 structures
80-89%: Consolidation with communicative pair/group activities; continue A2 exposure
70-79%: Targeted practice on weak grammar areas; form study groups for peer support
55-69%: Intensive focus on past tense, 3rd person -s, and question formation
<55%: Individual tutoring; revisit basic vocabulary and fundamental grammar; consider L1 support

Teacher Implementation Strategy

Before the Test:

  • Review A1 vocabulary and common structures (be, present simple, past simple)
  • Practice with similar test formats and time management
  • Create a supportive testing environment with minimal distractions
  • Clarify instructions in students' native language if needed

During the Test:

  • Allow 50 minutes for completion (approximately 1.5 minutes per question)
  • Monitor for understanding but do not provide content assistance
  • Encourage students to attempt all questions
  • Collect writing samples for qualitative assessment

After the Test:

  • Provide individual feedback focusing on strengths and growth areas
  • Use error analysis to inform targeted instruction
  • Celebrate improvements and progress toward A2
  • Recommend next steps based on score ranges above
